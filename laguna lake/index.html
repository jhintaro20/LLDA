    About This System

    This interactive dashboard presents water quality monitoring data collected by the Laguna Lake Development Authority (LLDA) for Laguna de Bay.

    About the Water Parameters

    The system uses key water quality parameters to help users understand the condition of Laguna de Bay and assess its suitability for aquatic life, fisheries, and environmental management.

    • Dissolved Oxygen (DO): Indicates how much oxygen is available in the water. Higher DO levels generally support healthier aquatic organisms.
    • pH Level: Shows whether the water is acidic or alkaline. Extreme pH levels can stress or harm aquatic life.
    • BOD & COD: Measure the amount of organic and chemical pollutants in the water. Higher values may indicate pollution and reduced water quality.
    • Turbidity: Reflects water clarity. High turbidity can limit sunlight penetration and affect aquatic ecosystems.
    • Temperature: Influences dissolved oxygen levels and the metabolism of aquatic organisms.
    • Nitrate & Phosphate: Nutrients that support plant growth but may cause algal blooms and eutrophication when present in excess.

    By visualizing these parameters on the map and charts, the system helps users quickly identify areas of concern and compare water quality conditions across monitoring stations.
